# The Darkening of Mirkwood — Adventure Extract

**Source:** TOR (The One Ring) — Darkening of Mirkwood **Authors:** Gareth Ryder-Hanrahan & Francesco Nepitello **Context:** Chronicle Sim — Event inspiration, NPC templates, plot structures

---

## Campaign Overview

The campaign spans 30 years (2947–2977) and tells the story of the slow fall of Mirkwood under the shadow of Dol Guldur. It is a tale of resistance against an inevitable defeat—heroes can delay the doom but hardly prevent it. The focus lies on personal tragedy: What can the heroes save when the Great is already lost?

- **Theme:** Light vs. Darkness—both literal and symbolic. The Lamp of Balthi is the central artifact.

- **Tone:** Increasingly dark. Early years offer hope; later years are marked by plague, betrayal, and loss.

---

## Era 1 — The Last Good Years (2947–2950)

*The final years before the shadow returns. The Woodmen increase in number, but the outskirts of Dol Guldur begin to stir.*

---

### Year 2947 — The Wizard's Man

**Premise:** The heroes encounter Beran the Watchful, one of Radagast's scouts, heavily poisoned by spiders. He carries a warning: Orcs have re-occupied Fenbridge Castle, an outer bastion of Dol Guldur. Before losing consciousness, he hands over a crumpled note with a map.

**Key NPCs:**

- **Beran the Watchful** — Woodmen scout in Radagast's service; key witness to the Orcs' return.

- **Dagmar** — Leader of the warriors from Tyrant’s Hill; servant of Mogdred. She lies about her intentions, wanting to take Beran back and destroy the message.

- **Mogdred (Lord of the Tyrant’s Hill)** — Mysterious master of the tower on Amon Bauglir; claims to be Ingomer’s son; key antagonist of the entire campaign.

- **Radagast the Brown** — Receives the heroes in Rhosgobel; deeply concerned about the Orc news.

**Main Conflict:** Should the warning about the Orcs get through or be suppressed by Dagmar and Mogdred? Mogdred wants to gain time to decide for himself which side to take.

**Twists:**

- Dagmar lied: She didn't protect Beran but captured him—he escaped her.

- Mogdred is Ingomer’s lost son "Ingold," who claims to have been a slave in Dol Guldur.

- Mogdred's former loyalty to the Necromancer is unclear—twist in Year 2959.

---

### Year 2948 — The Folk-moot at Rhosgobel

**Premise:** Representatives of all Woodmen houses gather at Rhosgobel for the folk-moot. Three important motions are up for vote: the admission of Ceawin the Generous from the East Bight, the construction of Black Tarn Hall by Amaleoda, and—as a dramatic disruption—Mogdred’s demand for a seat on the Woodmen Council.

**Key NPCs:**

- **Ingomer Axebreaker** — Chieftain of Woodland Hall; oldest advisor; father of Mogdred (Ingold).

- **Ceawin the Generous** — Young, golden-blonde leader of the East Bight settlers.

- **Amaleoda, Shieldmaiden** — Leader of the Black Tarn kin.

- **Mogdred** — Appears wearing a dragon-helm; reveals himself as Ingomer’s son; throws Orc heads before the assembly as "proof" of his protection.

**Outcomes Table:**

| **Motion**   | **Accepted**                                           | **Rejected**                                                    |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Ceawin**   | Sunstead is established; Woodmen stronger in the East. | Ceawin returns a failure; settlers flee to Dale.                |
| **Amaleoda** | Black Tarn Hall is built and grows.                    | Amaleoda remains alone; more Woodmen seek Mogdred's protection. |
| **Mogdred**  | Fights Orcs, but causes problems.                      | Becomes a raider; endangers travelers and new settlements.      |

---

### Year 2949 — The Questing Beast

**Premise:** Thranduil hosts a royal hunt for the legendary white stags of Mirkwood. Mid-hunt, the heroes find clues about the missing Elven archer Ruithel—and discover a dangerous feud between her and exiled Dwarves.

**Key NPCs:**

- **Ruithel** — Elven archer; carries *Penbregol* (Mithril bow); captured by Dwarves due to a misunderstanding.

- **Polin and Pomin** — Brothers and exiled Dwarves from the Greydelve; semi-mad; they hold Ruithel because they (falsely) associate her bow with the shaming of their chieftain.

- **Frár the Beardless** — Leader of the Greydelve exiles.

**Penbregol — The Bow of Sudden Fury:** Elven bow with Edge 8; always allows an additional opening volley.

---

### Year 2950 — Secrets Buried

**Premise:** Radagast takes the heroes in a mysterious bird-boat to explore southern Mirkwood—something dark is taking root in the abandoned Dol Guldur.

**Key NPCs:**

- **Duskwater** — The oldest River-maiden; confirms the awakening of evil.

- **Halbrech the Wineseller** — The spy! Formerly an agent of the Enemy in Thranduil’s court; he tried to destroy evidence.

**High Keep Clues:**

- Elven knife in an Orc corpse—someone from Thranduil’s realm was here recently.

- Secret chamber with documents: military intelligence on Lake-town and the Woodland Realm.

- Bloody pages describing Thranduil's defenses—there is a traitor in the Elven court!

## Era 2 — The Return of the Shadow (2951–2960)

*Three Nazgûl return to Dol Guldur. The fortress is slowly rebuilt.*

---

### Year 2951 — The Helm of Peace

**Premise:** Ceawin the Generous commissions a magnificent steel helm to appease the spirits of the old Northmen barrows. The heroes must deliver it, but they are ambushed by bandits.

**Twist:** If the heroes fail, Ceawin wears the helm and becomes cursed, acting as an instrument of the Wights in subsequent years.

---

### Year 2952 — The Staff of the Roadwarden

**Premise:** Bofri son of Bofur seeks help to explore Millfort, an old Dwarf-fort on the Old Forest Road, to recover the Staff of the Roadwarden. A giant spider sleeps atop the tower.

**Key NPCs:**

- **Tauler the Hunter** — Giant spider, spawn of Shelob; loves gems and consumes their light.

- **Duskwater** — Her corruption begins this year.

---

### Year 2953 — The Beast of the Forest

**Premise:** The Werewolf of Mirkwood is back. It is an evil spirit from the Elder Days, a servant of Sauron that inhabits wolf bodies.

**The Lamp of Balthi:** Celebrimbor created two crystal lamps for Amon Lanc. One was lost to the Necromancer; the other, found by Gandalf, is the "Lamp of Balthi" in Woodmen-town. The Werewolf destroyed the second lamp and is now tortured by the light of the remaining one.

---

### Year 2954 — The Lost Path

**Premise:** Heroes must find a path through the Narrows of the Forest.

- **Tyulqin the Weaver** — Spawn of Shelob; master of illusions; she uses a "Valley of Illusion" to trap the heroes.

---

### Year 2955 — Treacherous Waters

**Premise:** A boat on the Dusky River is driven into the Dark Heart of Mirkwood by Duskwater. The heroes must escape while being hunted by spiders.

---

### Year 2956 — Murder in Lake-town

**Premise:** The Second Council of the North meets in Lake-town. An assassin stalks King Bard.

- **Hunald** — Woodman archer under a shadow-curse; tasked with killing Bard with Angmar-arrows.

- **Outcome:** If Hunald isn't stopped, Queen Una of Dorwinion dies instead, leading to a political crisis.

---

### Year 2957 — The Siege of Black Tarn Hall

**Premise:** Black Tarn Hall is besieged by supernatural weeds and Forest Goblins led by a Nazgûl (*Ghost of the Forest*).

- **Saruman** — Visiting Rhosgobel; only helps if proof of a Ringwraith is provided.

---

### Year 2958 — Saving the Maiden

**Premise:** Radagast reveals that Tyulqin holds Duskwater captive. The heroes must travel to the Spider Parliament and into Tyulqin’s cave to break the spell.

- **Outcomes:** If Duskwater is healed, the Black Tarn recovers. If she dies, a new, harsher maiden (*Ice-spear*) emerges.

---

### Year 2959 — The Horse-Lord's Daughter

**Premise:** Athala, a daughter of the Leofrings, seeks revenge on Mogdred. She reveals that Mogdred was not a slave, but a voluntary captain for the Necromancer.

---

### Year 2960 — The Death of Ingomer

**Premise:** Ingomer Axebreaker dies. A new War-leader must be chosen.

- **Mogdred** — Claims the title as Ingomer’s son.

- **Ceawin** — Also a candidate.

- **Outcome:** Choosing Mogdred is catastrophic, as he eventually leads the Woodmen into hopeless battles.

---

## Era 3 — The Gathering Gloom (2961–2966)

*Dol Guldur is fully garrisoned. Trolls, traitors, and the theft of the Lamp.*

---

### Year 2961 — Paying the Troll

**Premise:** Stone Trolls block the Elf-path. Frár the Beardless asks for help, but his kinsman Flor betrays them to the Shadow.

---

### Year 2962 — Honour Among Thieves

**Premise:** The dying outlaw Geirbald Kinslayer wishes to return home. He prophesies the Werewolf's death: *"The beast will die... in a place without shadow... in a time without hope..."*

---

### Year 2963 — The Anger of the Elvenking

**Premise:** Thranduil learns the Woodmen possess an Elven lamp and is outraged. Gandalf explains he hid it there because the Woodmen need it more for the coming war.

---

### Year 2964 — The Greydelve

**Premise:** With support from King Dáin, Frár attempts to reclaim the Greydelve. Heroes must protect him from mercenary treachery.

---

### Year 2965 — The Thief of the Lamp

**Premise:** Wayward Elves steal the Lamp of Balthi. Without its protection, Orcs begin raiding Woodmen settlements. The chase ends at the Skybridge-tree where the Werewolf and a Nazgûl await.

---

### Year 2966 — Return to Dol Guldur

**Premise:** Radagast discovers prisoners (and potentially the Lamp) are held at Fenbridge Castle. A daring rescue mission is required.

---

## Era 4 — The Years of the Plague (2967–2974)

*A cruel plague from Dol Guldur decimates the Woodmen.*

---

### Year 2967 — Treachery in the Woods

**Premise:** Halbrech the Wineseller returns and poisons Thranduil’s wine. The King falls into a coma until 2970.

- **The Plague:** Body parts "woodify" like fallen branches. Elves are immune; the Lamp of Balthi protects.

---

### Year 2968 — Nine in the Hall

**Premise:** Heroes are trapped in a longhouse in Sunstead. The host is possessed by the spirit of King Raegenhere and is killing guests one by one.

---

### Year 2969 — Wheels within Wheels

**Premise:** Prince Bain is captured by outlaws. A pretender named Girion (son of Valdis) rises in Dale. Valdis, now a vampire, is draining King Bard’s life.

---

### Year 2970 — Slaying the Forest Dragon

**Premise:** A Forest Dragon (a flightless long-worm) is attacking the north.

- **Loot:** The *Torc of Fram* and the *Cup of Friendship*.

---

### Year 2971 — The Dying of the Light

**Premise:** The Werewolf must be lured into a trap using the Lamp. Its light permanently burns away the Werewolf's spirit.

---

### Year 2972 — The Hame of the Vampire

**Premise:** Valdis has merged with the hide of a Great Vampire. Heroes must track her to the Mirkwood Mountains to save King Bard.

---

### Year 2973 — The Reach of the Shadow

**Premise:** Prince Bain is imprisoned in Castle Orlmond. Heroes must prevent a civil war in Dale.

---

### Year 2974 — Beorn's Quest

**Premise:** Beorn leads heroes into the Misty Mountains to be tested by *The Hunter* (Béma/Araw). If they pass, they receive a permanent hope bonus.

---

## Era 5 — The Darkening of Mirkwood (2975–2977)

---

### Year 2975 — The Last Folk-moot

**Premise:** Thranduil demands the Lamp. Heroes must decide:

1. **Leave it:** Best chance for Woodmen, but Lamp is at risk.

2. **Give to Thranduil:** Elves flourish; Woodmen fall to darkness.

3. **Send to Rivendell:** Lamp is safe; Mirkwood is lost.

---

### Year 2976 — The March of the Woodmen

**Premise:** A doomed campaign against Dol Guldur.

- **Result:** Depending on the heroes' success, they can buy a generation of peace or have the power of Dol Guldur broken until the War of the Ring.

---

### Year 2977 — Epilogue: The Passing of the King

King Bard dies in his bed; Prince Bain becomes King. The Nazgûl report to Sauron: Is the North strong or weak? The answer depends on the heroes' actions over the last 30 years.
